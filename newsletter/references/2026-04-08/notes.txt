# **Title: Art that moves the world**

**Thesis**:

The urgency to engage with art, practice visual literacy, form opinions and foster discussion, tangibly *improves* society. 

We are what we eat, we are what we see. The media we consume and our methods for making meaning *matter* because it applies to everything.

Visual language is inherently political. The way we react to advertisements, propaganda, architecture, etc. is a direct byproduct of our visual thinking or in some cases “non-thinking”

In defense of abstract art. Today’s society rigorously enforces what is “correct” and “incorrect” The patience to sustain open-ended thinking → engage in inconclusive but thought-provoking discussions about abstract art, calls us to behave differently than what’s normally expected. We lack the attention span to sit in front of a painting and form original thoughts. We don’t understand, there is no “right” answer, and that feels frustrating.

Abstract art, and our proactive engagement with contemporary ideas, pushes our society forward philosophically and artistically. 

The power of artists, is the power to imagine. The power of artists is to think creatively and propose a new way of seeing, creating, doing, living. All of which are necessary to shape a future that is better than what it is now. 

Art is dually a critique of the generation preceding it and an invitation for society to realize a new potential.

“But in the situations in which most people use photographs, their value as information is of the same order as fiction.” (22)

“An artist is predisposed to the idea that there are many possible interpretations of any given situation. When you and I study the same abstract painting, what I see is probably different from what you see. The emotions and memories that are evoked depend entirely on the individual and their situated context.

Intuitively, we all understand this. But all too often, we fail to exercise the necessary imagination to break out of the constraints of “single-right-answer” thinking.

Stepping into the shoes of others and deriving insights from their imagined experience is central to the artist’s ability to envision alternative futures. This reference-shifting enables the artist to better conjure counterfactuals and anticipate how things might play out differently for different individuals in different contexts. It also helps the artist adapt to unanticipated challenges and generate solutions to problems as they arise.

As we shall see, a degree of artistic foresight is hugely beneficial to the entrepreneurial process. It allows the problem-solver to anticipate the possible destinations to which their solution might take them, as well as to communicate their ideas to others. Moreover, it teaches them to revel in the journey and evolve in tandem with challenges.”
