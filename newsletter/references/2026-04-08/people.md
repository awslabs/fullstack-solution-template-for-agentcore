# People

- **Antanas Mockus** — Mayor of Bogotá (1995–97, 2001–03). Mathematician and philosopher who used art and performance to transform civic behavior. Hired mimes to shame jaywalkers. Distributed 350,000 "thumbs up/thumbs down" cards to citizens. Proved that imagination is governance.
- **José Antonio Abreu** — Venezuelan economist and musician who founded El Sistema in 1975. Believed music education could rescue children from poverty. Built a national system of 400+ youth orchestras.
- **Doris Sommer** — Harvard professor, author of *The Cultural Agent*. Argues that artists and humanists are essential civic actors, not decorative additions to society.
- **Frank Gehry** — Architect of the Bilbao Guggenheim. The building itself became the argument that art reshapes cities.
- **Susan Sontag** — Relevant here for *On Photography* and the idea that images mediate (and sometimes replace) direct experience.
- **John Berger** — *Ways of Seeing*. Visual literacy as a political skill. Seeing is never neutral.
