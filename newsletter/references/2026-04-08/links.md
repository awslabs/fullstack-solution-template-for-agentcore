# Links & References

## Books
- *On Photography* by Susan Sontag (1977) — the mediation of experience through images; photography as a way of not-seeing
- *Ways of Seeing* by John Berger (1972) — visual literacy as political act; how images construct meaning
- *The Cultural Agent* by Doris Sommer (2014) — artists as civic actors; creative practice as social intervention
- *A Defence of Poetry* by Percy Bysshe Shelley (1821) — the poet as legislator of the world

## Case Studies
- **Bilbao Guggenheim** — Frank Gehry's museum transformed a declining industrial city into a global cultural destination. The "Bilbao Effect" demonstrated that a single building, a single act of artistic imagination, could reshape an economy and a civic identity.
- **El Sistema** — Venezuela's national music education program, founded by José Antonio Abreu in 1975. Gave instruments to children in poverty. Produced Gustavo Dudamel and hundreds of thousands of musicians. Proved that art education is social infrastructure, not luxury.
- **Bogotá under Antanas Mockus** — The mathematician-philosopher mayor who used mimes, art, and creative civic campaigns to reduce traffic deaths by 50% and homicides by 70%. Reimagined how citizens see themselves in a city through visual and performative interventions.
- **Hemispheric Institute / Cultural Agents** — https://hemisphericinstitute.org/en/enc07-work-groups/item/1072-enc07-cultural-agents.html — Doris Sommer's framework for artists as agents of social change
