# Newsletter Style Guide

## Voice
- First person, conversational but not casual
- Confident without being declarative — leave room for the reader
- Match the tone in `samples/` — read those first, always

## Structure
- No headers that announce what's coming ("In this issue...")
- Open with an image, moment, or observation — not a thesis
- Close with a single sentence, not a recap
- Short paragraphs. One idea per paragraph.
- Total length: 400–800 words unless the piece demands more
- No subtitles, but include breaks for sections (break)

## Language Rules

### Never
- "If X, not Y" sentence structure (e.g. "If art is about seeing, it's not about knowing")
- "In a world where..." openings
- "It's worth noting that..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- "Needless to say..."
- Rhetorical questions as transitions
- Exclamation marks (unless quoting someone)
- "We believe" / "We think" — just say it

### Avoid
- Adjective stacking ("beautiful, stunning, breathtaking")
- Adverbs that add nothing ("really," "very," "truly," "incredibly")
- Passive voice unless intentional
- Repeating the same word within 2 sentences (find a better word or restructure)
- Starting consecutive sentences with the same word
- Clichés about art ("feast for the eyes," "must-see," "hidden gem")

### Prefer
- Concrete over abstract
- Specific over general (name the painting, the room, the city)
- Short sentences for emphasis. Long sentences for rhythm.
- Present tense when describing art
- Active verbs: "the painting holds" not "the painting is holding"

## Quoting
- Attribute quotes naturally, not with "As X once said..."
- Prefer: `Berger writes, "Seeing comes before words."` 
- Or weave it in: `What Berger called "a way of seeing" is really a way of paying attention.`

## References
- Books: *Title* by Author (year)
- Films: *Title* (Director, year)
- Artworks: *Title*, Artist, year — medium if relevant
- Always include context for why you're referencing something
